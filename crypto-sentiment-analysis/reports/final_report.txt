## BEHAVIORAL FINANCE OBSERVATIONS

1. LOSS AVERSION AMPLIFICATION
   Traders in Fear mode take larger risks to "recover" losses,
   a classic behavioral finance error (break-even effect).

2. HERDING EFFECT
   Buy/sell imbalances during Greed show herd behavior.
   Most retail traders enter positions AFTER the move has occurred.

3. OVERCONFIDENCE IN GREED
   Win rates slightly decrease in Extreme Greed vs Greed,
   suggesting overconfidence leads to poor entry timing.

4. DISPOSITION EFFECT
   Traders hold losing positions too long during Fear (hoping for recovery)
   and exit winning positions too quickly during Greed.
